## üìã R&D CREDIT AUDIT DEFENSE

### Common IRS Audit Triggers:
1. Large increase in R&D credit from prior year
2. No documentation or poor documentation
3. Unusually high % of total expenses as R&D
4. Claiming 100% of wages (no allocation)
5. Retroactive time tracking (not contemporaneous)
6. Claiming routine maintenance as R&D

### Audit Defense Checklist:

**Before Filing:**
- [ ] Documentation reviewed by CPA
- [ ] Four-part test applied to all projects
- [ ] Time records contemporaneous
- [ ] Financial records reconciled
- [ ] Reasonable allocation percentages

**If Audited:**
- [ ] Engage R&D credit specialist immediately
- [ ] Gather all documentation
- [ ] Prepare project summaries
- [ ] Interview key employees
- [ ] Respond to IRS within deadlines

### Estimated Audit Costs:
- R&D credit specialist: $5,000 - $15,000
- Additional documentation: $2,000 - $5,000
- Potential adjustment/penalties: Varies

**Mitigation:** Do it right the first time with good documentation

## üéØ STARTUP SPECIAL PROVISIONS

### Payroll Tax Election (Section 41(h))

**Eligibility:**
- Less than 5 years gross receipts
- Less than $5 million gross receipts in current year
- Have payroll tax liability

**Benefit:**
- Can apply R&D credit to payroll taxes instead of income taxes
- Up to $250,000 per year
- Helpful when not yet profitable

**Forms:**
- Form 6765 (R&D Credit)
- Form 8974 (Qualified Small Business Payroll Tax Credit)
- Attach to Form 941 (Quarterly Payroll Return)

**Example:**
```
Startup Year 2:
R&D Credit: $15,000
Payroll Tax Liability: $8,000

Apply $8,000 to payroll taxes (immediate savings)
Carry forward $7,000 to future income tax years
```

### Documentation for Startups:
- Even more critical (smaller amounts, higher scrutiny)
- Document early-stage experimentation
- Keep failed experiments (shows uncertainty existed)
- Show evolution from concept to product
