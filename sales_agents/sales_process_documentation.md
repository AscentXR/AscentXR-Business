## 1. Sales Process Overview

### Process Flow Diagram
```
Lead Sources → Lead Capture → Qualification → Outreach → Meeting → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Close → Onboarding
    ↓              ↓            ↓           ↓         ↓        ↓        ↓           ↓           ↓         ↓
LinkedIn       Website      Scoring     Sequences  Booking  Custom    Pricing    Objection   Contract  Handoff
Events         Forms        Model       (Email,    System   Demo      Tiers      Handling    Signing   Process
Content        Chatbots     (0-100)     LinkedIn)           Scripts   Options                 (eSign)
```

### Process Stages & Definitions
| Stage | Name | Definition | Duration Target | Key Activities |
|-------|------|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Lead Created | New contact enters system | - | Data capture, initial scoring |
| 2 | Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL) | Meets basic criteria | 24h | Further qualification, list assignment |
| 3 | Sales Qualified Lead (SQL) | Ready for sales outreach | 48h | Personal research, outreach planning |
| 4 | Meeting Scheduled | First meeting booked | 1-7 days | Calendar coordination, preparation |
| 5 | Needs Analysis | Discovery completed | 1 meeting | Pain points, requirements, budget |
| 6 | Demo Completed | Product demonstration | 1-2 meetings | Custom demo, use case validation |
| 7 | Proposal Sent | Formal proposal delivered | 1-3 days | Proposal creation, pricing discussion |
| 8 | Negotiation | Terms discussion | 3-7 days | Objection handling, value justification |
| 9 | Closed Won | Contract signed | 1-2 days | Contract generation, e-signature |
| 10 | Onboarding | Implementation begins | Immediate | Handoff to CS, kickoff meeting |

## 2. Detailed Stage Processes

### Stage 1: Lead Creation & Capture

**Sources:**
1. **LinkedIn Prospecting**
   - Automated connection requests (50/day limit)
   - Content engagement (comments, shares)
   - Profile views with follow-up
   - InMail campaigns (premium accounts)

2. **Website Lead Capture**
   - Contact forms (instant qualification)
   - Demo request forms (priority routing)
   - Content download gates (nurture sequence)
   - Chatbot conversations (qualification)

3. **Event Follow-up**
   - Webinar attendees (48-hour follow-up)
   - Conference contacts (personalized reference)
   - Trade show leads (photo + notes capture)

4. **Referral Program**
   - Customer referrals (accelerated process)
   - Partner referrals (special pricing)
   - Employee referrals (bonus tracking)

**Capture Rules:**
- Minimum data: Name, Email, Company
- Preferred data: Role, Phone, LinkedIn URL
- Enrichment: Auto-lookup for company size, industry
- Duplicate check: 30-day window, merge if found

### Stage 2: Lead Qualification

**Scoring Model (0-100 points):**

**Demographic Score (30 points max):**
- Industry match (VR/AR, Training, Education): +10
- Company size (>500 employees): +8
- Decision-maker role (Director+): +7
- Geographic match (North America/Europe): +5

**Behavioral Score (40 points max):**
- Website visit duration (>5 minutes): +10
- Multiple page views (>3 pages): +8
- Demo request form submission: +15
- Content downloads (>2 items): +7

**Intent Score (30 points max):**
- Search terms ("VR training solutions"): +10
- Technology stack mention ("Unity", "Unreal"): +8
- Budget indicators ("training budget"): +7
- Timeline mention ("Q2 implementation"): +5

**Qualification Thresholds:**
- **Hot Lead (80+ points):** Immediate sales outreach (within 4 hours)
- **Warm Lead (50-79 points):** Nurture sequence (5-7 touches)
- **Cold Lead (0-49 points):** Newsletter only (monthly)

### Stage 3: Sales Outreach

**Multi-Channel Approach:**

**LinkedIn Sequence (14 days):**
```
Day 1: Connection request + personalized note
Day 3: Comment on their recent post/share
Day 5: Share relevant industry article
Day 7: InMail with specific value proposition
Day 10: Voice message (enterprise only)
Day 14: Breakup message or re-engagement
```

**Email Sequence (21 days):**
```
Day 1: Welcome + value proposition
Day 3: Industry insight/trend
Day 7: Case study (similar company)
Day 10: ROI calculator/template
Day 14: Social proof (testimonials)
Day 18: Limited-time offer
Day 21: Breakup email
```

**Response Handling Matrix:**
| Response Type | Action | Timeline |
|---------------|--------|----------|
| Positive interest | Schedule meeting | Within 2 hours |
| Request info | Send requested materials + follow-up | Within 4 hours |
| Not interested | Remove from sequence, add to newsletter | Within 24 hours |
| Wrong person | Request referral, update contact | Within 24 hours |
| Objection | Send objection-specific response | Within 4 hours |

### Stage 4: Meeting Management

**Booking Process:**
1. **Availability Matching:** Compare prospect availability with sales rep calendars
2. **Time Zone Management:** Auto-convert to prospect's time zone
3. **Buffer Time:** Minimum 30 minutes between meetings
4. **Preparation Time:** 15-minute prep block before each meeting

**Meeting Types:**
1. **Discovery Call (30 minutes):** Qualification, needs analysis
2. **Demo (45-60 minutes):** Product demonstration, use case validation
3. **Technical Deep Dive (60 minutes):** IT/technical team discussion
4. **Executive Briefing (30 minutes):** C-level decision makers
5. **Proposal Review (30 minutes):** Pricing, terms discussion

**Preparation Materials (Auto-generated):**
- Company overview (1-page)
- Industry challenges summary
- Relevant case studies (2-3)
- Competitive comparison
- Customized agenda

### Stage 5: Demo Execution

**Demo Framework:**
```
Part 1: Setup (5 minutes)
- Agenda review
- Attendee introductions
- Business context confirmation

Part 2: Problem Validation (10 minutes)
- Pain point exploration
- Current solution challenges
- Desired outcomes

Part 3: Solution Demonstration (25 minutes)
- Key features relevant to pain points
- Use case walkthrough
- ROI visualization

Part 4: Next Steps (10 minutes)
- Implementation discussion
- Pricing overview
- Decision process
- Follow-up actions
```

**Demo Success Criteria:**
- Prospect engagement score (0-10)
- Objection count and type
- Champion identification
- Decision timeline confirmation
- Technical feasibility confirmation

### Stage 6: Proposal & Pricing

**Proposal Generation Process:**
1. **Template Selection:** Based on deal size and complexity
2. **Customization:** Insert meeting insights, specific use cases
3. **Pricing Configuration:** Tier selection, add-ons, discounts
4. **Legal Review:** Standard terms, special clauses
5. **Delivery:** PDF + online interactive version

**Pricing Strategy:**
| Tier | Price Range | Target Customer | Key Features |
|------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Starter | $5k-15k/year | SMB, pilot projects | Basic features, limited seats |
| Professional | $25k-75k/year | Mid-market, department | Full features, API access |
| Enterprise | $100k+/year | Large organizations | Custom development, SLA, SSO |

**Discount Framework:**
- Annual prepayment: 10% discount
- Multi-year commitment: 15% discount (2 years), 20% (3 years)
- Non-profit/education: 25% discount
- Pilot program: 50% discount (3 months)

### Stage 7: Negotiation & Closing

**Common Objections & Responses:**
1. **"It's too expensive"**
   - Response: Value justification, ROI calculation, payment terms
   - Tactic: Break down cost per user, compare to alternatives

2. **"We need to evaluate other options"**
   - Response: Competitive comparison, differentiation, trial extension
   - Tactic: Offer head-to-head evaluation criteria

3. **"Timing isn't right"**
   - Response: Pilot program, phased implementation, success metrics
   - Tactic: Show cost of delay (opportunity cost)

4. **"We need executive approval"**
   - Response: Executive briefing, business case template, champion coaching
   - Tactic: Provide materials for internal presentation

**Closing Techniques:**
- Assumptive close: "When would you like to get started?"
- Alternative close: "Would you prefer the annual or quarterly plan?"
- Summary close: Recap value proposition and next steps
- Urgency close: Limited-time offer or implementation slot

### Stage 8: Contract & Implementation

**Contract Workflow:**
1. **Generation:** Auto-populate from proposal
2. **Review:** Legal checklist completion
3. **Signing:** E-signature workflow (DocuSign/HelloSign)
4. **Countersign:** Company signature
5. **Filing:** Digital storage + CRM update

**Implementation Handoff:**
- **Sales to CS handoff meeting:** Within 24 hours of signing
- **Implementation timeline:** 2-4 weeks typical
- **Success metrics definition:** Jointly with customer
- **Quarterly business review schedule:** Set for first meeting

## 3. Sales Tools & Templates

### Outreach Templates Library

**LinkedIn Connection Requests:**
```
Template A (Value-based):
Hi [Name], I see you're working on [specific challenge] at [Company]. We're helping companies like [similar company] achieve [result]. Would be great to connect and share insights.

Template B (Mutual Interest):
[Name], noticed your interest in [topic]. We're exploring similar challenges with [industry] companies. Would appreciate connecting to exchange perspectives.

Template C (Referral):
[Name], [Mutual Connection] suggested we connect regarding [topic]. I'd love to learn about your work at [Company].
```

**Email Templates:**
```
Initial Outreach:
Subject: Quick question about [their industry/challenge]

Hi [Name],

I came across your work at [Company] and noticed you're focused on [specific area]. We're helping companies like [similar company] achieve [specific result, e.g., 40% faster training completion].

Briefly, [value proposition - 1 sentence].

Would you be open to a 15-minute chat next week to explore if this could be relevant for [Company]?

Best,
[Your Name]

---

Follow-up (No Response):
Subject: Following up: [Original subject]

Hi [Name],

Just circling back on my previous email about [topic]. If now isn't a good time, no problem - I'll check back in a few weeks.

Alternatively, if you'd prefer not to be contacted, just let me know and I'll remove you from my list.

Best,
[Your Name]

---

Breakup Email:
Subject: Last try: [Value proposition]

Hi [Name],

This will be my last email about [topic]. I understand you're busy, so I won't follow up again.

If you do become interested in [solution] in the future, feel free to reach out.

Wishing you the best,
[Your Name]
```

```
IF Industry = "Manufacturing" AND Company Size = "Enterprise"
THEN Assign to [Enterprise Sales Rep]

```
When Deal Stage changes to "Proposal Sent":
